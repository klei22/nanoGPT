Read → Activate → Write, now with backprop + quantization modes

This keeps the original single-hidden-layer read / activate / write geometry, but now replays a real gradient-descent training process on two classic toy tasks. On top of the floating-point baseline, you can switch between post-training quantization (PTQ), quantization-aware training (QAT) with a low-precision forward pass, and a snap-after-each-step training mode that projects weights back onto a 1.58-bit ternary, 3-bit, or 4-bit grid. You can also switch the hidden activation among ReLU, ReLU², tanh, GELU, and SiLU, and slow down or speed up the Stage 6 replay from 0.1x to 2x.
